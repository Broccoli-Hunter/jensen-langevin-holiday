Let's get caught up...

It's been a couple of years since we've had time to update people with a Christmas card, so a few updates that aren't specific to 2022:

  • Laura left teaching and started a 2 year Business Information Systems diploma at Sask Polytech
  • Vincent assumed a Tech Lead then Dev Lead role at iQmetrix
  • Vincent and Laura are starting to think about wedding planning... again

Winter

2022 started off a little extra busy as Laura's classes moved to in-person format for the first time and she spent most of her days at the Moose Jaw Sask Polytechnic campus. Vincent was able to continue working at home for iQmetrix. We still found some time to enjoy fun winter actvities!

Kaylee's Grade 6 Extracurriculars

A lot of opportunity comes at grade 6 in Kaylee's school, and she took advantage! This was her first year of band (all students required to take it in grade 6), and she chose clarinet. She also helped with safety patrol, curled for the first time, joined a chess club, and played the badminton season!

Spring

Spring was really busy for us as Kaylee's extracurriculars continued and Laura's final projects were underway.

School Dances

Another school opportunity at grade 6 is school dances. Kaylee was very excited to go shopping for a nice outfit for her first dance. There was a second dance later in spring as well!

Regina Pride Parade

iQmetrix's "parent company" Chrysalis had a spot in the Regina Pride Festival's parade for the first time, so Vincent and Kaylee were with the Chrysalis crew this year! Laura had a school project demo event that day and couldn't join this year, but is looking forward to next year!

Archery at the Farm

Laura's final school project was to build (with a team of three others) a virtual reality archery game that people with vision loss could play. At the end of the project the team got together to celebrate by doing real archery at the farm one afternoon. The other team members had never done it before and we had just spent months creating a game to simulate it! While the target and equipment was still set up, we had some family time experiencing archery as well!

Summer

We had a fantastic summer with fun family time at home and away!

Laura Finished Diploma, Got Hired

In June, Laura completed her Business Information Systems diploma and convocated! She was also honored to be awarded Sask Polytech's 2021 Co-operative Education Student of the Year award for the 6-month work term she did building computer vision projects at Nutrien.

She was also hired soon in the summer into iQmetrix, so Vincent and Laura are now co-workers although on different teams. Laura works on a team that builds and supports mobile apps for the company.

Kipabiskau

Laura, Vincent, and Kaylee got a chance to get out of the city to Kipabiskau lake for a bit in August. We had a great time visiting with some of Laura's family, going on boat rides, and enjoying the campfire.

Car Accident/Replacement

One unfortunate event that happened this year was Laura's vehicle being rear-ended in June while waiting to make a left turn. Both vehicles involved badly damaged enough to be written off. On the bright side, she was able to find a new vehicle later in the summer and it's even a bit of an upgrade on what she was driving before!

Moose Jaw Fair & Queen City Ex

Kaylee was able to enjoy both the Moose Jaw Fair (June) and the Queen City Ex in Regina (August) this summer. Vincent enjoys going on some of the rides too, and Laura just takes pictures!

Autumn

Autumn is such a beautiful time of the year! It's nice to get back into routines after the summer break.

Kaylee's Grade 7 Extracurriculars

Kaylee is taking part in several activities this year! Outside of school, she is taking a 4-month photography/videography class. In school, she is doing curling, chess, and student leadership council. She also does street-crossing guard duty and helps the Kindergarten class at lunchtimes. Band maybe isn't considered extracurricular, but we're happy she chose to continue in band this year playing clarinet.

Jurassic Corn Maze

We had an opportunity to do the corn maze at Cedar Creek Gardens (south of Regina) with friends this year! Their maze is also a dinosaur-themed scavenger hunt, and it was a lot of fun!

Trip to Edmonton

In April, we were supposed to go to Edmonton for a weekend to see a performance from the choir Laura used to sing in. Two days before we were supposed to go, Kaylee and Laura tested positive for Covid, and Vincent followed a couple days later. Unfortunately, I had already talked up West Edmonton Mall to Kaylee and she was extremely disappointed that we weren't going.

Instead we planned a trip to Edmonton during the Thanksgiving weekend, mainly to see the West Edmonton Mall! This was Vincent's first trip to WEM, and the first one Kaylee could remember since she hadn't been since she was quite young.

We have pets!

Well, we had pets before too but we added a few during the pandemic! In 2020, we adopted 2 more cats (Dog and Kat) from Moose Jaw Humane Society. In 2021 we also adopted Frodo from SOS Prairie Rescue after meeting him at Excalipurr Cat Cafe in Regina. All of our pets get along great!

Happy Holidays!

We hope you enjoy the rest of this year with a safe and happy holiday season! And best wishes in 2023!
